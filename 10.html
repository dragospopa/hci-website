Iteration Process

In our initial phases, we designed two alternate layouts for our website (and each respective webpage). We then reviewed these ourselves, and more importantly, got potential users (i.e. classmates and colleagues) to review these designs for us, and ended up with a list of potential usability flaws and design oversights that we took into account and used considerably, in order to end up with our final website design. Below is a discussion of both the general design and layout, and also of the improvements made to each webpage in each iteration.

General Design - First Iteration

First Iteration: The general design and layout started with a large banner across the top of the page, and all the menu functions (Home, Jupiter Analysis, Forum, Signup, Login, Search) in a single menu directly below it. The content of the page was then located on the remaining page area, with a “Latest Forum Posts” area located to the right of all the webpages.

Feedback:

  • Too much space taken up by the banner across the top of the page. Make this smaller.
  • The menu is too large and crams too many functions into one space. Perhaps spread it out.
